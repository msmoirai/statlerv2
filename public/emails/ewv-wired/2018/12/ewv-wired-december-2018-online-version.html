Vinod Kulathumani, associate professor of computer science and electrical engineering, is part of a research team that has won the R&D 100 Award. The awards, known as the “Oscars of Innovation,” honor the top 100 proven technological advances of the past year as determined by a panel selected by “R&D Magazine.”
 

King named WVU’s third Marshall Scholar

May graduate Morgan King has been awarded a Marshall Scholarship, one of the country’s top awards, to study in the United Kingdom.
 

WVU selected to compete in third-straight Moon to Mars Ice and Prospecting Challenge

For the third straight year, a team from WVU will compete in the Moon to Mars Ice and Prospecting Challenge, a special edition competition under NASA’s Revolutionary Aerospace Systems Concepts – Academic Linkage brand of competitions.
 

Savage named under 35 innovator by MIT Technology Review

Saiph Savage, J. Wayne and Kathy Richards Faculty Fellow and assistant professor in the Lane Department of Computer Science and Electrical Engineering, has been selected by MIT Technology Review in Spanish as a 2018 Innovator Under 35 in Latin America.
 

Ye named Challenge Problem Winner in Artificial Intelligence for Cyber Security workshop

Yanfang (Fanny) Ye, assistant professor of computer science and electrical engineering, and her collaborators received the prestigious Challenge Problem Winner award in the Artificial Intelligence for Cyber Security Workshop.
 

Bhattacharyya named to endowed professorship

Debangsu Bhattacharyya, a professor in the Department of Chemical and Biomedical Engineering, has been named the GE Plastics Material Engineering Professor, effective January 1, 2019.
